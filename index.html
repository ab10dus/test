My Understanding & Core Assumptions

Before building a solution, it's critical to deconstruct the feedback and form a set of clear, testable hypotheses. This is my interpretation of the core problem.

"Too time-consuming"

This points to a broken process. It's a symptom of manual data entry, disconnected tools, and endless back-and-forth to chase missing information.

"Not the right value"

This is the critical insight. The process is delivering an answer to a question nobody is asking anymore. The "value" they need isn't a static report; it's dynamic decision-support for managing a complex connection queue.

My Testable Hypotheses

1. Process: The bottleneck is manual integration between siloed tools.
2. Value: "Lack of value" stems from the output being a static, non-actionable document.
3. Data: Engineers spend >30% of their time on low-value data entry and chasing applicants.
4. Tooling: There is no single source of truth for connection applications.
5. Strategic: A "single pane of glass" view would be a high-value, mission-critical tool for DNOs.
